This gRNA directs the Cas9 nuclease to the precise genomic location, where Cas9 induces a double-strand break (DSB). The cell then repairs the DSB through one of two primary mechanisms: non-homologous end joining (NHEJ) or homology-directed repair (HDR). NHEJ is an error-prone process that often leads to small insertions or deletions (indels), which can disrupt the gene's reading frame and result in gene knockout. HDR, on the other hand, requires a DNA template with homology to the regions flanking the DSB. If a donor template is provided, the cell can use it to repair the break, allowing for precise gene editing and insertion of new sequences. Viral vectors, such as adeno-associated viruses (AAVs), are commonly used for delivery, but they can elicit immune responses and have limited packaging capacity.
